EigenSafe: A Spectral Framework for Learning-Based Probabilistic Safety Assessment

Anonymous Authors
Paper

Figure 1. This cheetah learns to slide on its knees using EigenSafe.

Abstract

We present a framework for synthesizing a valid Stochastic Control Barrier Function (SCBF) through the lens of linear operator theory. By analyzing the Koopman-like operator associated with the evolution of the system's safety probability, we find that the dominant eigenfunction of this operator serves as a natural SCBF.

The values of the dominant eigenfunction explicitly reflect the long-term safety level of the queried state, making it an SCBF with physically interpretable values. We also propose the power-policy iteration algorithm, which provides a practical way of jointly computing the dominant eigenfunction and the backup policy.

Methodology

Our approach shifts the perspective from solving Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) to spectral analysis of linear operators.

The Koopman-like Operator

We define a linear operator $\mathcal{K}$ that describes the evolution of safety probability. The SCBF condition is derived from the spectral properties of this operator:

$$ \mathcal{K}[\phi](x) = \lambda \phi(x) $$

where $\phi(x)$ is the dominant eigenfunction and $\lambda$ corresponds to the decay rate of safety probability.

Results

We validated the method on a Wing-in-Ground (WIG) aircraft simulation, where ground effect creates complex aerodynamic interactions.
